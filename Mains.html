Vegetable Topped Pizza

Can be made Vegan!

Makes two large or four small pizzas, serves 4

Preparation time: 15 mins, 1 hour wait time for dough to rise

Cooking time: 15 mins

Difficulty: Easy

IngredientsMeasure
For the base
Strong white flour1 cup
Dried yeast1 teaspoon
Caster sugar1 teaspoon
Olive oil30ml, plus extra for drizzling
For the tomato sauce
Passata100mL
Fresh basil (can be replaced with dried oregano)1 tablespoon
Crushed garlic1 large clove
For the topping
Mozzarella (or vegan-style mozzarella)200g
Fresh cherry tomatoes100g
Vegetables of choice (olives, peppers, red onion, etc.)To serve (optional)
Fresh basil, oregano, chili oil, parmesan, etc.To serve (optional)

Method

  1. Put the flour, yeast and sugar in a large bowl. Measure 150ml of cold water and 150ml boiling water into a jug and mix them together – this will mean your water is a good temperature for the yeast. Add the oil and 1 tsp salt to the warm water then pour it over the flour. Stir well with a spoon then start to knead the mixture together in the bowl until it forms a soft and slightly sticky dough. If it’s too dry add a splash of cold water.
  2. Dust a little flour on the work surface and knead the dough for 10 mins. Put it back in the mixing bowl and cover with cling film greased with a few drops of olive oil. Leave to rise in a warm place for 1 hr or until doubled in size.
  3. Heat oven to 220C/200C/gas 9 and put a baking sheet or pizza stone on the top shelf to heat up. Once the dough has risen, knock it back by punching it a couple of times with your fist then kneading it again on a floured surface. It should be springy and a lot less sticky. Set aside while you prepare the sauce.
  4. Put all the ingredients for the tomato sauce together in a bowl, season with salt, pepper and a pinch of sugar if you like and mix well. Set aside until needed.
  5. Divide the dough into 2 or 4 pieces (depending on whether you want to make large or small pizzas), shape into balls and flatten each piece out as thin as you can get it with a rolling pin or using your hands. Make sure the dough is well dusted with flour to stop it sticking. Dust another baking sheet with flour then put a pizza base on top – spread 4-5 tbsp of the tomato sauce on top and add some sliced tomatoes and grated vegan cheese. Drizzle with a little olive oil and bake in the oven on top of your preheated baking tray for 10-12 mins or until the base is puffed up and the cheese has melted and is bubbling and golden in patches.
  6. Repeat with the rest of the dough and toppings. Serve the pizzas with fresh basil leaves or chilli oil if you like and sprinkle over parmesan just after baking.

Chickpea and Coriander Burger

Can be made Vegan friendly!

Serves 4

Preparation time: 15 mins

Cooking time: 10 mins

Difficulty: Easy

IngredientsMeasure
Tinned chickpeas1 can, drained
Lemon zest1, plus juice
Ground cumin1 teaspoon
Fresh coriander1 small bunch, chopped
Egg (or vegan egg replacer)1
Fresh breadcrumbs100g
Red onion1 medium, half diced, half sliced
Olive oil1 tablespoon
Burger buns of choice4
Tomato1 large, sliced

Method

  1. In a food processor, whizz the chickpeas, lemon zest, lemon juice, cumin, half the coriander, the egg and some seasoning.
  2. Scrape into a bowl and mix with 80g of the breadcrumbs and the diced onions.
  3. Form 4 burgers, press remaining breadcrumbs onto both sides and chill for at least 10 mins.
  4. Heat the oil in a frying pan until hot. Fry the burgers for 4 mins each side, keeping the heat on medium so they don’t burn.
  5. To serve, slice each bun and fill with a slice of tomato, a burger, a few red onion slices, and any other garnishes of choice.

Coconut Curry Noodles

Vegan friendly!

Serves 4

Preparation time: 15 mins

Cooking time: 25 mins

IngredientsMeasure
For the sauce
Vegetable oil1 tablespoon
Garlic cloves2, crushed
Red chilli½, finely chopped
Grated gingerone small piece
Mild curry powder1 tablespoon
Soy sauce1 tablespoon
Sriracha1 tablespoon
Coconut milk1 400g can
For the tomato sauce
Passata100mL
Fresh basil (can be replaced with dried oregano)1 tablespoon
Crushed garlic1 large clove
For the topping
Mozzarella (or vegan-style mozzarella)200g
Fresh cherry tomatoes100g
Vegetables of choice (olives, peppers, red onion, etc.)To serve (optional)
Fresh basil, oregano, chili oil, parmesan, etc.To serve (optional)

Method

  1. Lightly oil a 20cm square tin with coconut oil.
  2. Line the base of the tin with baking paper.
  3. Chop dark chocolate and pistachios.
  4. Melt together chocolate, maple syrup and coconut oil in the microwave for 30 second bursts until mixture is smooth.
  5. Break the ginger nuts into small pieces and add, with dried cranberries and pistachios into the melted mixture.
  6. Combine melted mixture with dry ingredients.
  7. Spoon into the tin.
  8. Place in fridge for 2 hours or until mixture sets hard.
  9. Once set, cut into 25 small square pieces.
